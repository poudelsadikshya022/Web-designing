Buddha Jayanti

Buddha Jayanti is celebrated to mark the birth of Lord Buddha, the founder of Buddhism. It is observed on the full moon day of Baisakh. People visit monasteries, offer prayers, and spread messages of peace and compassion. .

Tihar

Tihar, also known as the festival of lights. It is celebrated for five days, honoring animals like crows, dogs, and cows, as well as brothers and sisters. People decorate their homes with lights, candles, and rangoli, and celebrate with music, dancing, and the beautiful tradition of Bhai Tika.

Chhath

Chhath is a major Hindu festival dedicated to the Sun God. Devotees fast and offer prayers near rivers and ponds during sunrise and sunset. It is especially popular in the Terai region of Nepal.

GhodeJatra

Ghode Jatra is a unique festival celebrated in Kathmandu Valley. It involves horse races and parades organized by the army. The festival is believed to drive away evil spirits.
